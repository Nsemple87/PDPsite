We are building a modeling space to empower innovative planning and co-design for the New Children's Hospital.The space will allow hospital personnel and patients to express their ideas about improvements in the hospital layout while using realistic modeling.

We are 15 people from Aalto University and Pace University who met and started working together through the Product Development Project. Results of our 8 months of work is going to be presented at the Project Gala May 15-16 in Aalto's Design Factory.

Model space

The actual-sized model room is used as a tool for designing and achieving the perfect setup and functionality for a high-dependency hospital room. Co-design methods are being used to ensure that every user group is taken into account.

1:10 scale model

The 1:10 model is a design tool that is used to complement the actual-sized model. When brainstorming, the small scale model allows us to quickly test out new ideas. This is really effective since any big changes done to the real-sized model will be more time consuming.

The 1:10 model allows us to brainstorm on ideas and quickly test them out whenever and wherever we are. It is not bound to a specific location, which means we do not always have to visit the actual-sized model to test our ideas. This is essential when working with other partners who contribute to the project but are not a part of our team. The small-scale model also lets us quickly test new ideas. Any changes and testing with the actual-sized model space is always time consuming. With the help of the 1:10 model we can test and generate more ideas in less time. The small-scale model is also used in the co-design workshops that we have arranged in the 1:1 model space. It helps the participants who are unfamiliar with the model space and project to quickly get a general idea and grasp of what we are trying to achieve.

New ideas

Doing extensive research is essential when working with any design task, more so when you’re unfamiliar with the field you’re supposed to work in. This is why we devoted a great deal of time in understanding the details of the bigger picture.

To fully understand our task, we needed to do research on everything from visiting a hospital, to the devices and procedures used. We started our research by investigating the patient journey. The patient journey was made by interviewing three nurses at the current Children’s Hospital. It helped us to form an idea of what the patient goes through during his or her visit to the hospital.

Viewpoint of the nurses: After the patient journey, we wanted to get insight of the issues from the nurses. We wanted to find out what the common procedures and problems in the space currently are. We used contextual inquiry to gather the information from the nurses. This method is combination of both observation and interview. We did the contextual inquiry with four nurses from the current Children’s Hospital. Focus was on the high dependency room. Three of the nurses were from the wards and one was from the policlinic.

We also investigated similar projects which led us to contact researcher Juha Kronqvist, from whom we got a great deal of insight on planning a hospital model space.
